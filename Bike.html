Bike rentals in Budapest

Five months bike rentals insights.

1.Introduction

In this analysis, we delve into the intersection of weather conditions and bike rentals, aiming to provide insights and solutions to one business question as follows: The primary objectives are as follows:

- Weather Impact on Lending:

  • Explore the relationship between weather variables and bike rentals.
  • Identify routes, times of the day, or specific periods less influenced by weather conditions.
  • Investigate potential factors contributing to decreased weather relevance.
Data source HUNREN.

2.Project Overview

2.1 Overview of the Dataset

The dataset used for this analysis is stored in an Excel files named Station_data.csv, Train.csv, weather-data.csv The data contains information about dates of bike rides, weather conditions, and routes for five consecutive months Jan to May.

The data contains information about dates of bike rides, weather conditions, and routes for five consecutive months Jan to May.

2.2 Data Cleaning and Preprocessing

The dataset, available on HUNREN, spans from Jan - 2015 to May - 2015.
Several data cleaning and preprocessing steps were undertaken to ensure the quality and usability of the dataset:
Duplicate Rows: Duplicate rows in the dataset were identified and removed.
Missing Values: Missing values, denoted as '-999' for some weather-related variables, were handled appropriately, considering their impact on the analysis.
Route Formation: A new variable, 'Route,' was created by combining 'start_location' and 'end_location' to facilitate route-based analysis.
Merging Station and Weather Data: To enhance the dataset, station data and weather data were merged with the training data based on common columns.

3. Methodology

The exploration of the dataset was conducted using SQL for initial insights into its structure and basic metrics. Python was employed for in-depth analysis, utilizing statistical techniques to identify relationships between variables. Tableau was then used to visualize and interpret the findings, creating dynamic representations. The integration of SQL, Python, and Tableau provided a comprehensive understanding of the dataset, enabling precise responses to the business questions.

3.1 Key Findings

  • Weather Impact: Analysis revealed that weather conditions have a notable impact on bike rentals. Specific routes, times of the day, or periods exhibit varying sensitivity to weather changes.
  • Optimal Ad Placement: Based on user engagement patterns, recommendations for optimal ad placement were identified. Key rental points during specific months were suggested to maximize ad visibility.
  • Route Popularity: Certain routes consistently ranked high in popularity, indicating user preferences. Understanding these patterns can inform business strategies for resource allocation and service enhancement.
  • Data Integration: Merging station, weather, and rental data facilitated a comprehensive analysis, providing valuable insights into the interplay of factors affecting bike rentals.

4. Results

4.1 Weather Impact on Lending

4.1.1 Weather and Bike Rentals Relationship:

  • Clear Weather Dominance: Bike rentals peak during clear weather conditions, indicating a positive correlation between favorable weather and user demand (see Figure 1).
  • Impact of Precipitation: Rental counts significantly decrease during precipitation, emphasizing the negative influence of rain or snow on bike usage.
  • Temperature Influence: Rentals are lowest during extreme temperatures (very cold: -15 to 0, hot: 20 to 30). Optimal rental counts occur in cold to moderate temperatures (0 to 20), suggesting a preference for milder climates (Figure 2).


4.1.2 Weather and Bike Rides Correlation Heat Map Insights (Figure 3 & 4):

  • Strong Negative Correlations: Notable negative associations with humidity, rain, and fog, suggesting users avoid biking in unfavorable conditions.
  • Weak Negative Correlation with Snow: Rarity of snow contributes to a weaker correlation.
  • Strong Positive Correlation with Temperature: Clear positive association between bike rentals and temperature, indicating higher demand in warmer conditions.
  • Figure 4: Shows Correlation Heat Map Analysis of Temperature and Wind Speed where with increased temperatures and decreased wind speed, bike rentals increase.

4.1.3 Time Buckets and Bike Rentals:

  • Temporal Shifts: Figure 5 depicts bike rentals across time buckets during the first five months.
  • Early Months (Jan-Mar) Peak: Peak rentals from 8 am to 16 pm signify a preference for daytime use.
  • Shift in Peak (Apr-May): In April and May, the peak shifts to 14-12, indicating changing user behavior.
  • Reduced Weather Correlation: The temporal peaks suggest less correlation with weather during these hours, highlighting other influential factors.
  • Figure 6: Illustrates the correlation betweeen bike rentals and day of the week and hour.

5.1.5 Conclusion for Business Problem 1: Weather Impact on Lending

My analysis has unveiled key insights into the intricate relationship between weather conditions and bike rentals:

  1. Weather Influence on Rentals:
    • Clear weather drives the highest rental counts, while precipitation, extreme cold, and heat contribute to decreased rentals. Moderate temperatures between 0 and 20 result in peak rental numbers.
  2. Correlation Analysis:
    • The correlation heatmap reveals a strong negative association between rentals and humidity, rain, and fog. Conversely, a positive correlation exists with temperature, emphasizing weather's impact on rental patterns.
  3. Time Buckets Analysis:
    • Peak rental counts during specific time buckets indicate that rental patterns are less influenced by weather changes during peak hours between (08:00 to 16:00).
